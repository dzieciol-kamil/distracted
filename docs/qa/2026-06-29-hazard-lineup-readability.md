# QA Scenario: Hazard lineup readability and import artifacts

## Goal

Verify that all hazards in the controlled visual QA lineup are large enough and clean enough for a player to recognize before reaching them.

## Preconditions

- Existing artifact available: `qa-artifacts/visual-qa/hazards.png`.
- Note: this PNG was not freshly generated by the 2026-06-29 run because current capture commands failed or hung. Treat it as supporting visual evidence, not a fresh pass.

## Steps

1. Open `qa-artifacts/visual-qa/hazards.png`.
2. Inspect every hazard in the 3x3 lineup generated from `src/scripts/qa/visual_qa.gd`.
3. Compare against the visual QA rule: hazards must be recognizable before the player reaches them.

## Expected Result

- Every hazard is recognizable at QA capture scale.
- Labels are legible enough to identify which hazard is being reviewed.
- Imported models do not show black broken-looking fragments or texture artifacts.

## Actual Result

- Several hazards are extremely small in the lineup and are hard to identify from the capture.
- The train/vehicle area shows black jagged-looking artifacts around parts of the model.
- The label text below small hazards is also too small to be a useful QA aid.

## Impact

The player-facing risk is poor hazard readability: if the same scale/readability appears during gameplay, the player cannot fairly recognize the threat in time. The QA-facing risk is that the controlled lineup does not give a reliable visual review surface for small hazards.
