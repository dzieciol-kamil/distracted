# QA Scenario: Phone overlay road strip is obstructed by controls

## Goal

Verify that phone mode hides most of the road while leaving a fair, readable lower road strip for peripheral awareness.

## Preconditions

- Existing artifact available: `qa-artifacts/gameplay-smoke/03_phone_overlay.png`.
- Existing artifact available: `qa-artifacts/visual-qa/ui_phone.png`.
- Note: these PNG files were not freshly generated by the 2026-06-29 run because current capture commands failed or hung. Treat them as supporting visual evidence, not a fresh pass.

## Steps

1. Open `qa-artifacts/gameplay-smoke/03_phone_overlay.png`.
2. Inspect the visible road strip at the bottom of phone mode.
3. Check whether the player can still read the lane and immediate road state while the phone is open.
4. Compare against the product rule: the phone should interfere with play, but the situation must still feel fair.

## Expected Result

- Phone overlay covers most of the screen.
- A narrow bottom road strip remains visible.
- Controls do not cover the key readable road area.
- The visible strip communicates lane position and immediate hazards clearly enough for fair play.

## Actual Result

- The bottom road strip is visible, but the large left/stop/right controls cover much of it.
- In the smoke artifact, the center `STOP` control overlays the road directly, reducing the useful visible area.
- Bright green side areas dominate parts of the visible strip, making the road context less readable.

## Impact

The phone mode may become unfair rather than tense: the player is asked to keep peripheral road awareness, but the UI controls obscure the exact area that should preserve that awareness.
